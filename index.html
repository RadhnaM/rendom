ThEoRy

Computer-generated random numbers are pseudo-random because they rely on algorithms and initial seed values. Human brains, on the other hand, are believed to be capable of generating more 'genuine' randomness due to their unpredictability. In higher mathematics and quantum information science, randomness remains a profound and elusive concept, with quantum mechanics potentially providing a source of true randomness.

As computing continues to evolve, the question of whether machines can ever truly produce randomness akin to that found in nature remains unanswered, with quantum computing leading the quest for solutions.
